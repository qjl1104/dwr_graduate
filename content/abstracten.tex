\begin{abstracten}\setlength{\baselineskip}{20pt}%\renewcommand{\baselinestretch}{1.0}
Audio classification is a crucial research area within artificial intelligence and digital signal processing. While classical deep learning models have made progress, they face challenges like massive parameter sizes and computational bottlenecks. Quantum audio processing has emerged as a potential solution; however, existing methods often overlook the temporal evolution of audio signals and struggle with training deep circuits on Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum (NISQ) devices. Therefore, under the stringent parameter and computational constraints of NISQ devices, overcoming the bottlenecks of insufficient temporal feature representation and training instability to achieve high-accuracy, lightweight audio classification has emerged as a critical challenge urgently requiring resolution in this field. This thesis investigates audio classification based on quantum time-series encoding and its optimization strategies. The main contributions are as follows:

(1) A highly parameter-efficient Quantum Time-Frequency Analysis Neural Network model based on Quantum Time-Series Encoding is proposed. To address the limitations of traditional encoding in capturing dynamic temporal features, a quantum time-series encoding scheme based on dual-qubit sequence entanglement is designed. It globally entangles Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients (MFCCs) with audio time indices, aiming to solve the challenge of losing temporal correlations when mapping features into a high-dimensional quantum space. Experimental results on the GTZAN dataset demonstrate that the model achieves 75\% accuracy with only 33 trainable parameters, exhibiting clear advantages in parameter efficiency and feature capture capability.
	
(2) A cross-paradigm knowledge distillation optimization method, structured around a ``classical teacher-quantum student" framework, is introduced. To alleviate the difficulties of variational quantum circuits in falling into local optima and the ``barren plateau" phenomenon on NISQ devices, this method employs a pre-trained classical Convolutional Neural Network as a teacher network. It extracts high-dimensional acoustic features as continuous probability distributions to guide the optimization of the compact quantum student model. This transfer mechanism enables the quantum student model to effectively simulate the decision patterns of the teacher model within a minimal parameter space, achieving efficient cross-paradigm knowledge transfer. Experimental results show that this mechanism effectively narrows the generalization gap of the pure quantum model, increasing test accuracy to 76.17\% and the recall rate to 82.67\%, while demonstrating improved convergence speed and noise robustness.
