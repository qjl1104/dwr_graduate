\begin{abstracten}\setlength{\baselineskip}{20pt}%\renewcommand{\baselinestretch}{1.0}
Audio classification is an important research direction in the field of artificial intelligence and digital signal processing. In recent years, although the classical deep learning model has made progress, it also faces the challenges of large parameters and computing power bottlenecks. With the development of quantum computing, quantum audio processing came into being. However, the existing methods often ignore the temporal evolution characteristics of audio signals, and face difficulties in deep line training on noisy medium-scale quantum ( NISQ ) devices. Therefore, how to break through the bottleneck of insufficient time series feature representation and unstable training under the strict parameters and computing power constraints of NISQ equipment, and realize high-precision and lightweight audio classification has become a key problem to be solved in this field. In this paper, the audio classification and optimization strategy based on quantum temporal coding are studied, which are studied from two dimensions : feature extraction network construction and cross-computing paradigm training strategy. The main research results of this paper are as follows : 
 
(1) An efficient quantum time-frequency analysis neural network model based on quantum temporal coding is proposed. Aiming at the limitation that traditional quantum coding is difficult to capture the dynamic timing characteristics of audio, a quantum timing coding method based on double qubit sequence entanglement is designed. The Mel frequency cepstrum coefficient ( MFCC ) is globally entangled with the audio time index to solve the problem of correlation loss when the audio timing characteristics are mapped to high-dimensional quantum space. On this basis, an end-to-end network architecture including quantum convolution layer, quantum pooling layer and measurement module is constructed. The experimental results on the GTZAN dataset show that the model achieves a classification accuracy of 75\% with only 33 trainable parameters, showing obvious advantages in parameter efficiency and feature capture ability.

(2) A ``classical teacher-quantum student'' cross-computing paradigm knowledge distillation optimization method is proposed. In order to alleviate the problem that variational quantum circuits are easy to fall into local optimum and ``barren plateau'' on NISQ devices, this method uses pre-trained classical convolutional neural network as a teacher network, extracts the continuous probability distribution of high-dimensional acoustic features, and reversely guides the quantum student model with fewer parameters to optimize. This transfer mechanism enables the quantum student model to effectively simulate the decision-making rules of the teacher model in a very small parameter space, thereby achieving cross-paradigm knowledge transfer. The experimental results show that the mechanism effectively reduces the generalization gap of the pure quantum model. The accuracy of the model on the test set is improved to 76.17\%, the recall rate is 82.67\%, and it shows good consistency in terms of convergence speed and anti-noise robustness.
\end{abstracten}
